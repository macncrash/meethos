# Ideas — where earth3 goes next

The current slice proves the spine: galaxy → solar → Earth in one experience, with a living
civilization. These are the bets that make it a *game* and make it "never built before."

## Near-term (extends the slice)

1. **Surface regime** — dive below the globe into a SimCity-scale region: districts, roads,
   resources. The fourth link in the chain; the civilization layer becomes its zoomed-out summary.
2. **Coupled scales** — what happens at one scale shows at another: a star's main-sequence aging
   dims the planet; an asteroid in the orrery actually strikes the surface; an ice age thins the
   cities. Today the regimes are independent; coupling them is the magic.
3. **Save / share a world** — a world is a seed + a snapshot (ethersim's zod-schema + deep-link
   approach). Share "year 4,820 of seed 0xC0FFEE" and someone else continues it.

## The differentiators

4. **True f64 floating-origin** — collapse the cross-faded hand-offs into one literal, unbroken
   zoom across 30 orders of magnitude. ethersim scaffolded the hook; this is the headline feature.
5. **Deep civilization** — economies, migration pressure, conflict, tech trees, beliefs that spread
   (a memetics layer). Watch history *emerge* rather than scripted, world-wide and consistent.
6. **Many worlds** — the galaxy has billions of stars; let more than one grow life. A galactic-scale
   civilization map where you zoom into whichever story is most interesting.
7. **Players / agency** — nudge a civilization, seed a colony, divert a comet. SimCity's "god with
   a budget" but the budget is physics and the map is everything.

## North star

A single, seamless, seed-shareable universe you can zoom into anywhere — from spiral arms to a city
street — where every scale is simulated and the scales talk to each other. Pick **one** of the above
per milestone; #1 (surface regime) and #2 (coupling) are the highest-leverage next steps.
